Step 8 of 12 · Shape & feel

The texture of the system.

Buttons, corners, density. Small choices that compound into how the whole thing feels.

Button shape

Rounded

Soft 6–8px radius. Universal default.

Pill

Fully rounded ends. Friendlier, can feel toy-like in dense UIs.

Sharp

Zero radius, often uppercase. Decisive, editorial, brand-led.

Text-link primary

No buttons at all — arrows and text. Editorial, content-led products.

Density

How tightly information packs together. Affects table rows, list items, navigation spacing.

Comfortable

Relaxed rows. For consumer apps and most SaaS.

Compact

Tight rows. For data-heavy admin, dashboards, IDE-like tools.

Shadow philosophy

How the system handles depth.

Soft

Light, neutral shadows. The default for most products.

Warm-tinted

Brand-tinted shadows. For editorial, considered products.

Hard-edged

Solid offset shadows, no blur. For bold, graphic, brutalist styles.

No shadows

Borders only. Flatter, more editorial. For minimal systems.

Motion personality

How transitions feel. Hover the previews to see them animate.

Snappy

Fast, precise, ease-out. 120–180ms. Best for technical, productivity.

Considered

Smooth, deliberate. 200–280ms. The default for most products.

Bouncy

Springy, playful. Best for friendly products. (Off by default; turn on in step 11.)

Luxe

Slow, fading. 320–480ms. Best for luxurious, contemplative products.

Step 11 of 12 · Negotiable rules

Some things are usually banned. Want any?

Each is normally flagged as "AI slop" or a bad habit. Off by default. Toggle on if it's right for this project.

Glassmorphism
Frosted-glass effects. Often a tell, but legitimate for floating navigation, sidebars, modals over content.
Title
Gradient text
Coloured gradients on type. Fine for marketing display, weak on UI metrics.
Coloured glow
Neon halos. Right for cyberpunk, gaming, music apps. Wrong otherwise.
Centred everything
Centring as default. Works for landing pages, weak for product UI.
Bounce / elastic motion
Springy easing. Right for friendly products, dated for serious ones.
Pure black / white
#000 and #FFF as canvas. Usually feels harsh; some brands use them deliberately.
Aa
Inter as default body
Inter is everywhere — readable but generic. Allow only if it genuinely fits.
Side-tab accent cards
Coloured stripe down a card's side. The #1 AI tell — but occasionally intentional in dashboards.
Icon tile above heading
Icon-in-rounded-square stacked over heading. The universal AI feature-card template.
Modal-heavy interactions
Modals for non-destructive actions. Usually a sign of UX laziness.
Nested cards
Cards inside cards inside cards. This is a hard ban for you — not toggleable. Shown for completeness.
Always-on rules. Cards-inside-cards banned. WCAG AA contrast. Body text 14px minimum. Animate transform/opacity only. Empty/loading/error states required for every component. If dual mode, both equally polished from day one.
